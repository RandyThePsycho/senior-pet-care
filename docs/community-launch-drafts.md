# Community Launch Drafts

These drafts are for careful, manual community work. Senior Pet Care must be presented as an observation and vet-conversation tool, not a diagnosis product.

## Launch Goal

- Primary metric: 7-day reassessment rate from email-captured users.
- Secondary metric: email capture rate by source.
- Research metric: repeated needs from pet parents, especially what they want help deciding next.

## Universal Rules

- Read each community's rules before posting.
- Do not post the same text across many communities.
- Do not lead with a link when joining a discussion.
- Disclose affiliation when sharing PawCheckin.
- Use one link only when it directly helps the thread.
- Do not give medical advice, treatment advice, prognosis, or emergency triage.
- If a pet seems suddenly worse, distressed, or uncomfortable, suggest contacting a licensed veterinarian.

## Quora Answer Pattern

Use for questions like "How do I know if my old dog/cat is still comfortable?" or "What should I track before a vet visit?"

Draft:

There is no single online answer that can tell you what is happening medically. A veterinarian is the right person for that.

What can help before the appointment is organizing observations in a consistent way. For senior pets, I would usually write down:

- appetite and drinking
- pain or discomfort signs
- hygiene or accidents
- mobility
- engagement or confusion
- more good days vs. hard days
- any sudden changes

This makes the conversation less rushed and helps you ask clearer questions. I am building PawCheckin, a small quality-of-life tracker for this exact purpose. It is not diagnostic, but it can help create a printable summary before a vet visit:

https://pawcheckin.com/?utm_source=quora&utm_medium=answer&utm_campaign=feature_a_launch
