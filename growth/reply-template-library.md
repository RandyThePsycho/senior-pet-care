## 1. Senior Dog Arthritis / Mobility

Short:
That sounds really hard, especially when mobility changes make normal routines feel uncertain. I would track what changed, when it happens, whether stairs/floors/transfers are harder, and how your dog seems after activity.

Medium:
Mobility changes can be tough because memory gets fuzzy fast. For the next vet conversation, it may help to write down trouble standing, slipping, stairs, bathroom breaks, sleep after activity, appetite, and whether your dog seems uncomfortable or suddenly worse. That does not diagnose the cause, but it gives your vet a clearer pattern.

No-link:
If pain, weakness, collapse, or distress changes suddenly, I would contact a licensed vet rather than waiting to track it.

## 2. Cat Dementia / Night Crying

Short:
Night crying can be exhausting and worrying. I would note when it happens, how long it lasts, appetite/water, litter box changes, confusion, sleep, and whether anything seems to soothe or worsen it.

Medium:
For an older cat, patterns around night crying may be more useful to a vet than a one-time description. A small log can include time of night, vocalizing, appetite, drinking, litter box use, hiding, confusion, mobility, and any sudden changes. It is not a diagnosis, but it can make the vet conversation more concrete.

No-link:
If your cat seems distressed, painful, weak, or suddenly worse, a licensed vet is the right next step.

## 3. Senior Cat Not Eating

Short:
Eating changes are worth writing down because they can blur together quickly. Note what changed, when it started, how often it happens, water, litter box changes, weight concerns, and energy.

Medium:
I would be careful about trying to interpret appetite changes online, but a short record can help your vet: what food is offered, what is actually eaten, timing, water intake, litter box changes, vomiting, hiding, discomfort, and whether the change was sudden or gradual.

No-link:
If the appetite drop is sudden or your cat seems weak, painful, or distressed, I would contact a licensed vet promptly.

Medium:
For a vet visit, it can help to bring a simple pattern: when accidents happen, whether your pet seems aware, water intake, mobility, discomfort, sleep changes, and whether cleanup is affecting comfort or skin. That is observation, not diagnosis.

No-link:

## 7. Product Confusion

Medium:
