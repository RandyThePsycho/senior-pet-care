## YouTube Shorts / Reels / TikTok Scripts

### Script 1: Senior Dog Weekly Check

Hook:
If your older dog seems "a little different," write down the same seven things for one week.

Body:
Comfort, appetite, water, bathroom habits, mobility, mood, and whether there were more good days than hard days.

Close:
It is not a diagnosis. It is a clearer note to bring to your veterinarian.

`/guides/senior-dog-quality-of-life-checklist`

### Script 2: Senior Cat Changes Are Easy To Miss

Hook:
Senior cats can hide changes until the pattern is already pretty clear.

Body:
Track appetite, water, litter box, grooming, jumping, hiding, sleep, and good vs. hard days.

Close:
Use the notes to ask better questions at the vet, not to guess what is wrong.

Link target:
`/guides/senior-cat-quality-of-life-checklist`

### Script 3: Before The Vet Visit

Hook:
Before a senior pet vet visit, do not just say "something feels off."

Body:
Write what changed, when it started, how often it happens, and one or two examples.

Close:
That kind of note can make the conversation calmer and more specific.

Link target:
`/guides/older-pet-vet-visit-notes`

### Script 4: Good Days Vs Hard Days

Hook:
One hard day does not always tell the whole story.

Body:
For older pets, the weekly pattern matters: comfort, routine, appetite, mobility, mood, and good days vs. hard days.

Close:
Bring the pattern to a licensed veterinarian if you are worried.

Link target:
`/tools/senior-pet-quality-of-life-calculator`

### Script 5: The 7-Day Follow-Up

Hook:
The first note is useful. The second note is where the pattern starts.

Body:
Check the same senior-pet categories again after 7 days. Did comfort, appetite, mobility, bathroom habits, or mood change?
