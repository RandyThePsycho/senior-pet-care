## Draft Angles Ready For Tomorrow

### Night Waking / Caregiver Sleep

Helpful frame: separate the pet's pattern from the caregiver's sustainable care
capacity. Track timing, settling, appetite/water, bathroom trips, medication
timing, mobility, and the caregiver's sleep.

No-link reply stance: "Bring a 7-day pattern to your veterinarian; do not try
to diagnose from one night."

### Low Appetite / Late Eating

Helpful frame: track the 24-hour total and the timing pattern separately.
Include texture, chewing/dropped pieces, water, stool/vomit, weight, energy,
and any post-dental or kidney/GI context.

No-link reply stance: "This is not about forcing food; it is about giving the
vet a cleaner pattern."

### Mobility / Slipping / Lifting

Helpful frame: note what routes fail, what surfaces are involved, whether the
dog can potty safely, whether the caregiver can lift without risk, and whether
medication timing changes wobbliness.

No-link reply stance: "Caregiver safety belongs in the vet/neuro follow-up
conversation."

### Quality Of Life / Good Days Versus Hard Baseline

Helpful frame: occasional good moments matter, but the useful question is
whether the pet returns to comfort after hard periods and how much of the day is
now distress, confusion, pain-like behavior, cleanup burden, or exhaustion.

No-link reply stance: "No online reply should decide timing; bring the pattern
to a licensed veterinarian."
